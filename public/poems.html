Songs, Poems, And Other "Oems"





Wholehearted Youthfulness

Decades of tapes in a closet, 
 Forgotten, dust-laden reels, 
As life tumbles forward, without pause.

The lip of your skin,veiled now—
I glimpse the tenderness of my own youth, 
Dripping softly from the corner of your eye

Soft skin beneath a lonesome blanket— 
I stand, trembling, trying to contain 
What once was uncontainable

I must.
Now.
But time is patient.

Yet there is a light—
Though I am blind,
I feel it warm the hollow of this heart.

And someday, when the moon learns to love again, 
Perhaps I will, too.



As you look into the eyes—
blue and broken, eyes drowning in sadness so deep
that words have no place, no purpose.
What took over sits silent,

those eyes cry out for you, endlessly.
They weep through the night, flailing,
hiding beneath the cold evening,
until sleep falls on them—
semi-golden, exhausted,
still trembling.



The day I ventured to Eden, it was cold,
cold and wet, the kind that sinks into your bones.
I was tired when my visitor came,
tired and buried beneath linen covers
that the moonlight swept me under.

Unrecognizable, that face—
the figure, the body towering over me.
It was Thursday, I remember.
A long, dark day that demanded nothing,
yet still, I retired. No purpose behind it,
just another wasted hour.

The figures it showed me were lost to reason,
mere shadows, no value.
The moon pressed into my chambers,
thawing darkness whispered into my ear,
and it hurt—knowing what I knew.

That your lifeless body poured herself a drink,
I remembered; Your mouth opened,
but neither I nor the moon listened.

Euphoria struck at three o’clock—
a sudden flood of bliss.

But it was dark, I remember. Cold and damp.
You left that morning, and with you went my sanity.
Incomprehensible were the words,
both then and now.



The journey was painful—
facts, feats, and everything in between.
Damp and wet, a place I had already seen.
They say the West is best,
but only for the sane.

Goodbyes are harder when you’re there to witness them,
harder still when you’ve already left.
Hermits, those who wander,
will never understand.
Knowledge holds the key,
but the depths bring only madness.

It was cold and damp,
cold and damp again.
And on the floor, a pile of forgotten memories—
discarded, soaked.
Cold and damp,
cold and damp,
a rhythmic tapping on the thin glass window,
permeable, fragile.



On the day I lost my mind,
I made peace with my self-doubt.
The air hung cold, cool, and dusky,
as I rose from my bed.
Shadowy figures lingered at the edges,
but none dared to approach.
A whirlpool spun violently in the corner,
pulling at the stillness.
Then silence—
until a screech tore through my ears.
The blasphemous world crept closer,
