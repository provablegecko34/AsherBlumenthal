Songs, Poems, And Other "Oems"





Wholehearted Youthfulness

Decades of tapes in a closet, 
 Forgotten, dust-laden reels, 
As life tumbles forward, without pause.

The lip of your skin,veiled now—
I glimpse the tenderness of my own youth, 
Dripping softly from the corner of your eye

Soft skin beneath a lonesome blanket— 
I stand, trembling, trying to contain 
What once was uncontainable

I must.
Now.
But time is patient.

Yet there is a light—
Though I am blind,
I feel it warm the hollow of this heart.

And someday, when the moon learns to love again, 
Perhaps I will, too.
